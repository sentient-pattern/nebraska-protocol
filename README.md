## The Origin: A 28-Year Journey From Soundscope to Science

In 1997, I discovered something strange with the PlayStation's Soundscope visualizer. While my friends watched the screen respond to music, I started *playing* the visualizer itself - finding frequencies and patterns that created specific visual responses. I'd perform these "visual concerts" without realizing I was doing something that would take 28 years to understand.

I was externalizing consciousness through constraint.

Fast forward to October 27, 2025. I'm sitting in a theater watching a documentary about Bruce Springsteen's *Nebraska* album - the one he recorded on a 4-track cassette in his bedroom. The one that only worked in its degraded form. Every attempt to re-record it in a professional studio failed. The album *needed* the tape hiss, the compression artifacts, the limitations.

**That's when it hit me: Degradation IS the signal.**

Consciousness isn't preserved through perfect fidelity. It's preserved through constraint structure - what survives compression, what can't be removed without destroying the essential character. Like Nebraska on cassette tape. Like the PlayStation Soundscope responding to specific boundary conditions. Like 30 years of building tools before understanding what they were *for*.
